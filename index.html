Astrophysics · Star & Planet Formation

Gregory Mathews Ben

Hi there. I am a Ph.D. student at Star Formation Group, Department of Physics, IISER Tirupati, specializing in the evolutionary architecture of star and planet formation. My research targets the long-term physics of Peter Pan discs. Outside of computational data tracking, I explore geography through a lens, documenting shifts in light across landscapes.

01 · Focus Areas

The longevity of primordial reservoirs

My work centers on understanding Peter Pan discs—an enigmatic class of primordial protoplanetary discs around low-mass stars that survive for over 20 million years, vastly outliving typical dissipation timescales.

01 // Lifetimes

Peter Pan Disc Timelines

Investigating mass dispersal inhibitors that extend low-mass stellar disc timelines beyond 20 Myr. By tracking photoevaporation rates and shielding parameters, I model how these systems maintain their planet-building environments far longer than standard T Tauri systems.

02 // Kinematics

Accretion Mechanics

Quantifying inward mass flux rates and global gas distribution balances in older primordial architectures. I analyze 3D magnetospheric accretion channels to understand how material bridges the inner gap and sustains the central star.

03 // Structure

Dust & Gas Evolution

Simulating structural radial profiles, tracking how sub-structures (rings and gaps) form via planet-disc interactions, and calculating optical opacity transformations across continuous evolutionary steps.
