Protoplanetary Disc Evolution

My research focuses on understanding the survival and dissipation of protoplanetary discs-the rotating environments of gas and dust where planets are born. By combining large-scale cluster surveys with multi-wavelength time-series data, I investigate the underlying physical mechanisms that allow certain rare stellar systems to preserve their discs for more than tens of millions of years. These rare long-lived disks are called "Peter Pan" disks (clever naming; like the character these disks refuse to grow up!!). More disk lifetime implies more time available for planet formation and these disks challenge our standard models of disk evolution.

FOCUS 01

Mapping Disc Survival

While most discs dissipate within 10 million years, my work involves conducting comprehensive blind surveys of young stellar clusters in the solar neighborhood. By tracking these populations across extended timeframes (up to 100 Myr), I estimate the disk lifetime across different wavelengths. This revealed more disk lifetime for longer wavelengths; implying a inside out clearing scenario. The stars that retain for longer timescales where low mass stars; I found that as age increases; disks where found only across the low mass stars. This sample of long-lived disks provides critical targets for follow up spectroscopic and time domain studies to understand the physical processes that allow them to survive for so long. [Read more]

FOCUS 02

Dynamic Variability in Older Discs

Using mid-infrared data from NEOWISE, I track how the structural profiles of these old disc systems change over time. Investigating this dynamic variability provides critical clues about active processes operating in the disc, such as shifting structural warps or the presence of newly formed, embedded planetesimals moving through the dust. The sample consists of 120 old disk systems from our blind survey and 13 known peter pan disks. I identified 32 sources exhibiting significant variability and majority of these variables cluster in the 10-20 Myr age range, suggesting a sharp decline in dynamic disk activity after 20 Myr. Majority variables show irregular varaiblity, consistent with variable extinction or inner disk warping induced by embedded planetesimals. I am yet explore their optical variability using TESS and ZTF.

FOCUS 03

3-D Modelling of long lived "Peter Pan" discs

Using MCFOST, I model these rare discs to get insight about their three-dimensional structure which might explain their longevity. I make use of optical and infrared observations to constrain the physical properties of these systems. I'm also employing MCMC techniques (e.g., emcee, Nautilus) to sample the posterior probability distributions of the model parameters. Patiently waiting for exciting results!!
